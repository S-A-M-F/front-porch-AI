# Rawhide — What's New (Nightlies)

These notes feed the in-app "Update Available" dialog for Rawhide / cutting-edge builds.

## Recent improvements

- 🔀 **`/turnorder` — control who speaks when in a group** — tired of characters answering in a random order? In any group chat, type **`/turnorder roundrobin`** for a predictable cycle, **`/turnorder random`** to shuffle, or set an exact sequence with **`/turnorder Mara, Kai, Rin`** (anyone you leave out is added to the end, so nobody drops out of the rotation). `/turnorder` on its own shows the current mode and order. No more spamming `/speak` to wrangle the order.

- 🔥 **NSFW Enhancement (arousal) now works in group chats** — the arousal/lust toggle used to only exist in 1:1 chats, so in a group there was literally no way to turn it on and arousal never tracked. It now appears in the group sidebar, and flipping it correctly applies to **every** member (not just one), so lust starts moving for the whole cast.

- 🕯️ **Scene time no longer resets when you turn a 1:1 into a group** — converting a solo chat to a group (or collapsing back) used to snap a fixed scene time like "evening, Day 1" back to "morning, Day 1". Now the time of day and day count carry across the conversion intact, even with passage-of-time turned off.

- 💾 **Smarter automatic backups** — your database is now backed up every 30 minutes and kept on a two-tier rolling schedule: the most recent snapshots *plus* one backup per day for the last 7 days. So whether you need to undo something from this afternoon or roll back to yesterday's data, there's a restore point waiting — and old ones are pruned automatically so they never pile up. Manage and restore them on the Backups screen.

- ⚠️ **Cloud Sync is deprecated** — Cloud Sync has proven unreliable across devices (it could even bring deleted characters back to life), so it's now marked deprecated and **will be removed in a future release**. It still runs for now, but the supported way forward is the improved local backups above for safekeeping, and **Card export / import** to move characters or groups between devices. A clear notice now appears on the Cloud Sync screen.

- 🧬 **Turning a 1:1 into a group (and back) now keeps everything** — converting a solo chat to a group, or collapsing a group back to a 1:1, used to quietly reset a bunch of your character's state. Now it all carries across, both ways: the relationship/mood/needs, **character evolution** (their developed personality & scenario), **active quests/objectives**, **author's notes**, the **NSFW-enhancement toggle and arousal**, chaos mode, passage-of-time, and the AI's **deep memory** of the conversation — so after a conversion the cast can still recall specific earlier events that have scrolled off-screen, in *both* directions. Your character keeps who they've become no matter how the scene's cast changes. (One small caveat: a group's evolution *counter* still resets if you fully close and reopen the group before collapsing — the evolved traits themselves are kept.)

- 🗣️ **`/speak <name>` now works in full groups** — the macro that makes a present character jump in and take their turn right now isn't just for Lite NPCs anymore. In a full group chat, type **`/speak Mara`** and Mara takes the next turn immediately instead of waiting for the rotation (partial names work too; an unknown or ambiguous name tells you who's actually in the scene).

- 🔗 **Exported Group Cards remember each member's original character** — when you export a group and later re-import it, the members now reconnect to the matching characters in *your* library instead of arriving as untraceable copies. So if you ever shrink that re-imported group back down to a 1:1, it lands on the real library character. (Older cards still import fine; they just fall back to matching by name.)

- 👥 **`/join` and `/exit` now work inside full group chats too** — the cast macros are no longer 1:1-only. In a group, type **`/join Mara`** to bring a character in as a full member (no `--full` needed — a group has no "lite" tier, so it's always full), and **`/exit Mara`** to remove a member. When someone leaves they now **say a proper goodbye in their own voice** instead of just vanishing — and an **UNDO** appears for a few seconds: tap it and the goodbye is removed and they're back *exactly* as they were, with every bit of their bond, mood, needs, evolution, quests and memory intact (nothing is actually deleted until you carry on). Remove members until only one is left and the chat **automatically collapses back into a clean 1:1** with that original character — no leftover duplicate copies. The old "Add Character" button is gone from the group sidebar (freeing up space); just type `/` to see every command and pick `/join`.

- 🔄 **Redo your main character's reply even after a Scene Guest has answered** — when a Lite NPC chimes in right after your main character, you used to be stuck: the regenerate button only ever lived on the *very last* message, so once the guest spoke you could no longer redo the main character's response. Now a regenerate icon appears on the main character's bubble too. Tap it and the guest's now-stale reply is cleared away, your main character's response is rolled back and regenerated (relationships, mood and Needs revert correctly, exactly like a normal redo), and then the guest decides for itself whether to chime in again — speaking up only if the new reply actually concerns it, instead of blindly repeating.

- 🪟 **One chat screen for everyone — solo, guests, and groups** — single chats, 1:1 chats with Scene Guests, and full group chats now all use the *exact same* screen, header, and sidebar (the SillyTavern way). A solo chat is simply a cast of one; add a guest or another character and the same window grows a little **roster of avatars** at the top of the sidebar — tap any face to focus that character and see *their* emotion, bond, trust, needs, fixation and growth. The header shows a single portrait for a solo chat and a neat stack of portraits once there's more than one speaker. Nothing about your existing chats changes under the hood — your solo characters' relationships and Needs behave exactly as before; this just unifies the look and lets you move smoothly between a quiet 1:1 and a crowded scene without ever switching screens.

- ⌨️ **Type `/` to see every chat command** — no more memorizing the macros. The moment you type `/` in the message box, a little list pops up right above it showing every command (`/create`, `/join`, `/speak`, `/exit`, `/scan`, `/expression`) with a one-line description of what each does; it filters as you keep typing (`/sp` → just `/speak`), and you can **tap any one to drop it into the box** ready for you to fill in. It tucks itself away as soon as you start typing your actual message.

- 👋 **Invite your existing characters into a 1:1 chat with `/join`** — Scene Guests aren't limited to made-on-the-spot newcomers anymore. Type `/join` to pop up a searchable picker of your whole character library and tap whoever you want to walk into the scene — or type `/join Mara` to bring them in instantly by name (it opens the picker if the name is ambiguous). Whoever you pick makes a natural entrance written from the current conversation and their own character card, then sticks around in their own bubble and speaks for themselves, just like a `/create`d guest. The picker never lists your current main character (you can't invite the host into their own chat) or anyone already in the scene. And because they join as a *guest*, none of their own Realism, mood, or Needs comes along — your main character's simulation stays untouched. Mix and match freely: several guests can share a scene, each taking their own turns and chiming in when it's natural. Want a specific guest to speak *right now* instead of waiting for them to chime in? Type **`/speak <name>`** (or just `/speak` for the most-recent guest) — and if you mistype the name, it tells you who's actually in the scene. And if you `/exit` a guest by mistake, an **UNDO** appears for a few seconds: tap it and the goodbye message is removed and the guest is back exactly as they were — same memories, same personality growth.

- 🧠 **Scene Guests now remember** — the lite characters who drop into your 1:1 chats finally have a memory of their own. When a guest takes a turn, Front Porch AI quietly files away what just happened under *that guest's* name, and on their later turns it recalls the relevant past moments — so a returning guest can pick up threads from earlier in the scene (and even from other chats they've been in) instead of acting like they just walked in. Each guest's memories stay theirs: your main character's recollections never leak onto a guest, and a guest's never bleed onto your main. (Uses your existing RAG memory setting — nothing new to turn on.)

- 🌱 **Scene Guests now grow on you** — the lite characters you drop into a 1:1 chat no longer stay flat. As a guest keeps showing up and taking turns, Front Porch AI quietly lets them develop — picking up new traits and reflecting how the scene has shifted, exactly the way your main characters do with Character Evolution. Their growth sticks to that conversation, takes effect on their later turns, and never touches your main character's mood, relationship, or Needs. (Character Evolution must be enabled in settings; guests evolve on the same "every N messages" cadence as everyone else.)

- 🪄 **Scene Guests now arrive on their own** — when your main character keeps mentioning the same new named person in their narration (a sister, a regular at the bar, a recurring rival), Front Porch AI quietly notices and pops up a gentle "“Mara” has joined your chat with Aria." offer. One tap on **Add as Scene Guest** turns them into a real, self-speaking character (using the same instant generation as `/create`); tap **Ignore** and they stay background narration and won't be offered again. It only watches your 1:1 chats, checks in occasionally (not every line), and never suggests your own character or someone already in the scene — so it stays out of the way until there's genuinely a new face worth bringing to life. In a hurry (or testing it on a chat where a side character already keeps coming up)? Type **`/scan`** to run the check right now instead of waiting for the next automatic pass.

- 🎭 **Scene Guests — drop a second character into a 1:1 chat** — type `/create Mara: a wandering bard` mid-conversation and a brand-new character is generated on the spot, joins your scene in their *own* message bubble with their own name and avatar, and speaks for themselves through the same AI engine. They become a real, saved library character (so you can reuse them later). Use `/exit` (or `/exit Mara`) and your main character narrates them leaving. Scene Guests are intentionally lightweight: they don't carry Realism, mood, or Needs tracking, so adding one never disturbs your main character's relationship, emotions, or simulation. (More automation — auto-arrivals, memory, and per-guest depth — is planned for later phases.)
- 📤 **Export characters as JSON (and import them back)** — characters now have an "Export JSON" option in their right-click menu, alongside "Export PNG". It writes a standard Character Card V2 `.json` file (the same format shared around the community and used by SillyTavern and others), so your cards travel without an image when you don't need one. Importing a raw `.json` card now works too — previously the picker accepted `.json` files but only PNG cards actually loaded. Full round-trip: everything you can export comes back in.

- 📥 **Bulk-import Backyard AI characters (files *or* folders)** — the "Import BYAF" picker now lets you select **multiple `.byaf` files at once** (a single file still shows the full preview). And **"Import Folder" now imports both V2 PNG cards and `.byaf` files together**: it shows a breakdown of what's in the folder with independent checkboxes ("12 V2 PNG cards" / "5 Backyard AI files" + "also import chat history"), so a mixed folder imports exactly what you choose — no surprises. Everything runs through one progress bar with success/fail counts.

- 🛠️ **AI Character Creator backend & model setup overhauled** — both Pseudo-Remote and KoboldCpp (local) backends now have their own dedicated sections with proper `.kcpps` preset + model override selectors, a colored status indicator dot (Stopped/Starting.../Loading model.../Ready, pulsing during transitions), and a Start/Stop button. The Stop button checks both backend services so it stays enabled when switching tabs. KoboldCpp adds a foldable "Extra Settings" panel (kcpps, GPU info, GPU layers, context size, KV quantization, auto-configure) folded by default. Executable discovery uses `BackendManager.backendPath` like the rest of the app. Remote (API) + oMLX now share a unified searchable model picker inline. Model selection is finally consistent and usable across all backends.

- 🎭 **AI Character Creator fully restored — it generates and saves real characters again** — The creator's setup screens had been stripped down, and underneath it was worse: "generating" a character actually produced a hardcoded placeholder (no AI was ever called) and the final Save step never saved. It's all back and working: **Quick**, **Guided**, and **Automated** setup with every option (appearance builder, archetype presets, personality/backstory/NSFW trait chips, world-lore URL + file attachment, the "magic wand" description writer), the **Realism** screen (now with custom needs baselines + decay rates), and the **Review** screen (avatar regenerate/crop, editable card, lorebook cherry-pick). The three modes now share one polished, unified look — same bubbles/cards, each tinted a different accent (Quick=green, Guided=teal, Automated=amber) — and avatar image-gen settings are reachable right from the creator. Generated cards also now use {{char}} (not the literal name) and reliably include a first message.

- 📁 **Subfolders no longer create phantom duplicate characters** — Making a subfolder and dragging a character into it used to spawn a duplicate on the parent screen — and deleting that "duplicate" deleted your real character. Fixed: a character lives only where you put it, with no phantom copy. Large libraries also reload a little faster when you organize folders.
