# Rawhide — What's New (Nightlies)

These notes feed the in-app "Update Available" dialog for Rawhide / cutting-edge builds.

## Recent improvements

- 🎭 **Web app: the AI Character Creator gets all three modes** — building a character with AI on the web now matches the desktop's **Quick**, **Guided**, and **Automated** flows in one stepped wizard. Quick is a one-line concept; Guided walks you through vision + appearance/personality/world fields with tap-to-add suggestion chips; Automated gives you archetype presets and a full appearance / measurements / backstory / relationship builder. You can **drop in wiki/lore links or a .txt/.md/.pdf file** so the character comes out canon-accurate, and when an image backend is set up it **auto-generates a portrait** at the end, just like the desktop. (Web UI.)

- 👥 **Web app: create group chats** — the library has a **New Group** button that opens a wizard: pick two or more characters from a searchable avatar grid, name the group, choose a turn order, and **Create & open** drops you straight into the group chat. (Web UI.)

- 🎚️ **Web app: generation settings are real sliders now** — the Settings → Generation section matches the desktop's sampler panel: **Temperature, Min-P, Repeat Penalty, Rep Pen Tokens, XTC Threshold, XTC Probability, Max/Min Output Tokens, and Context Size** are all sliders with editable value boxes. These settings are shared with the desktop app — tune them in either place and the other follows. (Web UI.)

- 🖼️ **Web app: full image-generation settings** — the Models → Image Generation panel now has the same controls as the desktop: pick your **backend (Remote API / Local A1111 / Local Draw Things)** with the right fields for each (A1111 URL vs Draw Things gRPC host+port), an **art-style** selector, and **size, sampler, steps, CFG scale, and negative prompt**. (Web UI.)

- 🎨 **Web app: customize your chat colors** — a new **Chat colors** section in Settings lets you set your bubble + text, the character's bubble + text, and the **dialogue / action** role-play colors, with a live preview. Your own messages now get the same "dialogue"/*action* coloring the character's do. (Web UI.)

- ✍️ **Web app: a better chat composer** — the message box **grows as you type** (then scrolls), **colors your dialogue and actions live** as you write them, and "smart" curly quotes are highlighted too — in your messages and the character's. (Web UI.)

- ⚡ **Web app: instant library sync + smoother chat** — add, rename, or delete a character/folder/group in the desktop app (or another browser tab) and the web library updates **near-instantly**, no refresh. And the chat no longer flickers or "reloads" while a reply streams or the Realism engine works. (Web UI.)

- 📊 **Web app: see current Needs in the chat sidebar** — the in-chat sidebar shows each character's **current Needs scores** (hunger, energy, social, and the rest) as level bars, in both 1:1 and group chats. (Web UI.)

- 📖 **Web app: Porch Stories grows up — write, read, and listen to whole novels** — the story tools on the web now match the desktop end to end. **Setup** gets quick-concept idea chips (with a Refresh reroll), the full genre/mood/style lists, length/pace/dialogue/maturity options *with their descriptions*, per-character **roles** (Protagonist, Antagonist, Mentor…) and a persona role — and "seed from chats" / "include my persona" finally carry your real character-card details into the generator. The **bible dashboard** lets you assign a **TTS voice to each character**, edit act titles & descriptions (with their Convergence Points shown), and open a real **chat-history panel** (timeline, raw messages, redistill). The **structure** view shows each act's emotional-valence trajectory, scene casts, completion badges, and a **Rewrite scene** button; the **writer** color-codes each beat by type with pacing/valence and **Copy/Export** buttons. The **reader** is now an immersive paper **page-flip book** with a Table of Contents, prev/next, remembered reading position, and **Read to me** that narrates your story aloud with each character's own voice — plus optional ambient page-turn sound. And you can **export your finished book** as **plain text, Markdown, a proper EPUB eBook, or a stitched-voice audiobook (.wav)** right from the browser. (Web UI.)

- 🗂️ **Web app: organize your library like the desktop** — the web library is no longer read-only. **Make folders and subfolders**, **rename** and **delete** them, and **move characters in or out** by drag-and-drop or a tidy right-click menu. Each character card now has a full menu: **Edit, Duplicate, Export as PNG, Export as JSON, Move to folder, Remove from folder, Delete**. Turn on **Select** (or **Organize**) to multi-pick cards and **move or delete many at once**. Group cards get their own menu too — **Open, Export the whole group as a PNG, Extract its members into your library as individual characters, Delete**. Plus a **grid-size slider**, an **Import Date** sort, **folder-scoped search** (this folder / + subfolders / everywhere), and **Import a whole folder** of cards at once. (Web UI.)

- 🧠 **Web app: full Realism & Needs setup when you create or edit a character** — the web creator and editor now expose everything the desktop does instead of a stripped-down handful of toggles. Set the starting relationship (short/long-term bond + trust with the same tier names), starting emotion + intensity, time of day & day number, the optional NSFW-cooldown / Chaos / passage-of-time / Realism Verification (Director) features, and an opening quest. Plus the full **Needs Simulation**: per-need starting levels *and* decay rates for all seven needs, an "enjoys low hygiene" inversion, and a 1×–5× swing-strength dial. Best of all, characters you make on the web now start with the **exact same baselines** the desktop app would give them. (Web UI.)

- ✏️ **Web app: edit a character's Realism/Needs after the fact** — these seeds used to be invisible and unchangeable once a character existed on the web. Now the edit page loads and saves them, so you can tune a character's relationship, emotion, and needs anytime. (Web UI.)

- 📚 **Web app: richer lorebook editing + bulk import** — each lorebook entry now keeps its **Enabled** switch and **Sticky Depth** (these were silently dropped before), and you can **Import a lorebook JSON** (SillyTavern / Chub / Front Porch formats) to add many entries at once — in characters *and* worlds. (Web UI.)

- 🗺️ **Web app: the Worlds page now matches the desktop** — my shared lorebooks finally look and work like the real app. Each world is a **color-coded card** that shows the **linked character's avatar** (or a tinted initial), its **entry count**, and what it's linked to, topped by a **stats dashboard** (Worlds / Lore Entries / Linked Worlds). I can **Import a whole world from JSON** (SillyTavern / Chub / Front Porch) and **Export any world to JSON** with one tap — so moving worlds between machines, or sharing them, just works. (Web UI.)

- 👋 **Web app: alternate greetings & linked worlds are editable** — the character editor can now add/remove the alternate openings readers cycle through, and tick which **worlds/lorebooks** are attached to a character. A live **token estimate** badge shows roughly how big the card is as you type. (Web UI.)

- 🖼️ **Web app: crop avatars before you upload them** — adding a character avatar or expression image on the web now opens the same kind of crop tool the desktop has: drag to reposition, pull a corner to resize, or hit **Zoom Out (Pad)** to give a too-tight image some breathing room, then **Crop & Save**. No more uploading the raw, uncropped file. (Web UI.)

- 🎨 **Web app: macro highlighting + a full-screen field editor** — when I edit a character on the web, every long text box (description, personality, scenario, first message, example dialogue, system prompt, post-history, and each lorebook entry) now **highlights `{{macros}}`** like `{{char}}` and `{{user}}` so they're easy to spot at a glance, and each field has an **⤢ expand button** that opens a roomy full-screen editor for comfortable long-form writing. The token-estimate badge now counts exactly what the desktop counts (name, the seven text fields, and alternate greetings) and colors at the same thresholds, so the web and desktop numbers finally match. (Web UI.)

- 🔋 **Web app: tune Needs simulation mid-chat** — the chat side panel has a **Needs simulation** switch right next to Realism, and the low-needs warning bars turn red at the same critical point the desktop uses, per-character in groups. (Web UI.)

- ⚡ **Web app: One-Shot Eval, a scene-time strip, and evolution review in chat** — the chat side panel finishes catching up to the desktop. Flip **One-Shot Eval** to fuse the relationship + scene evaluations into a single LLM call and roughly double the Realism processing speed. The **Scene & time** section now shows a six-dot **dawn → night** strip so you can see the part of day at a glance. And under **Memory & evolution** there's a **Review / reset evolution** button: read the original personality & scenario side-by-side with the AI-grown versions, hand-edit them, or reset a character back to their original card — and in a group it all targets whichever cast member you're focused on. (Web UI.)

- 🧠 **Web app: watch the Realism Engine think** — when the AI is sizing up the scene, the web now shows the same live "engine is thinking" overlay as the desktop: a streaming read-out of the realism evaluation, the relationship/emotion/scene/trust stages, and a **Cancel Realism** button if you want to skip it. You'll also see when the Objective Engine is checking whether your goals/tasks got completed. (Web UI.)

- 🗂️ **Web app: the chat side panel matches the desktop** — the panels are now in the expected order (Author's Note up top, Current Fixation highlighted above the relationship stats, then Memory, Chaos, Objectives, Summary, and so on), and you can **drag the panel's edge to resize it** to your taste (it remembers the width). (Web UI.)

- 📥 **Web app: a real model manager** — the Models page now works like the desktop app. See your GPU and VRAM, tap a recommended-for-your-hardware chip to search, browse HuggingFace, and download with a proper queue: pause, resume, cancel, pause/resume all, and a live overall progress bar with per-file size and ETA. Delete installed models you don't want (the one in use is protected). Plus a fix so downloaded models always land in your real models folder. (Web UI.)

- 🧠 **Web app: pick your API model from a real list** — when you're on a remote backend (Nano-GPT, OpenRouter, etc.) the generation model is no longer a blank box you have to type into. Choose your provider from a dropdown (it fills in the address for you), then pick the model from a searchable list pulled live from the provider — hundreds of models, type to filter, with pricing shown. A **Test connection** button confirms your key works. (Web UI.)

- ⚡ **Web app: replies stream live again** — on a computer or phone, the AI's reply now appears word-by-word as it's written, and regenerating a message updates in place. Previously (over the remote/Tailscale connection) the chat could look frozen and you'd only see the new message after leaving and re-opening the conversation. (Web UI.)

Tap (or hover) any chip with an ⓘ to read the reason the engine gave for that change. And on the latest reply you can now hit **Manual Reprocess** to tell the Realism Director how the Needs *should* have changed (with a written critique) and re-run it — with a one-tap **Revert** if you don't like the result. (Web UI.)

- 🖥️ **Web app: the desktop browser now looks like the real app** — on a computer the library **fills the whole window** and the character grid scales to as many columns as fit, instead of a cramped strip floating in the middle. Characters and group chats are now **big cover-art cards** with tags and message counts (no more tiny circles). Desktop and mobile are now genuinely **separate layouts**, so improving one can't break the other — tap the **"Front Porch AI"** title anywhere to jump back to your library. (Web UI.)

- 📱 **Web app: a real phone experience** — the web UI no longer feels broken on iPhone/iPad. Content stays inside the screen (no more cut-off edges or sideways scrolling), the bottom toolbar and message box sit clear of the notch and home bar, and tapping a text field no longer zooms the page around. The whole app now picks up Front Porch's colors instead of looking flat and grey — chat replies color *actions* and "dialogue" the way the desktop does, and the persona list got real cards. The redundant "add to home screen / connect" nag is gone once you're already using it on your phone. (Web UI.)

- 📖 **Web app: write whole novels with Porch Stories** — the full story generator is now on the web. Create a story, walk the setup wizard (concept, genres/mood, POV, pacing, maturity, and optionally seed it from your existing character chats), then generate the story bible, act structure, and prose — or hit **Autopilot** and let it write the entire thing. Drill into the act → scene → beat structure to generate or rewrite any piece, read the finished book with per-scene read-aloud, and export to .txt/.md. Generation streams live progress so you can watch it work. (Web UI.)

- ✨ **Web app: create characters with AI** — the web library now has an **✨ AI Create** button next to ＋ Create. Describe a character (name + concept, optional personality keywords, optional mature-content toggle) and the AI writes the whole card — personality, example dialogue, lorebook, first message and more — then drops you straight into the editor to tweak and save. You watch it build step-by-step as it goes. Uses whatever LLM you've got running. (Web UI.)

- 🔊 **Web app: hear replies and talk back** — the web UI now has voice. Tap the **🔊** under any character reply to hear it spoken *on the device you're holding* (your phone or laptop, not the host PC), and use the **🎤** button by the message box to dictate instead of type. Voice uses whatever TTS/STT engine you've set up on the desktop app, and the controls only appear when those are turned on — the mic also needs a secure connection (Tailscale, ngrok, or localhost), so it won't show on a plain-LAN address. (Web UI.)

- 📲 **Web app: drop a generated image straight into your chat** — generate an image in the web UI's Image Generation panel and you'll now see an **Insert into chat** button next to Download. One tap attaches the picture to the latest message in your open conversation, where it renders inline — just like images do in the desktop chat. (Web UI.)

- 🖼️ **Turn on Image Generation right from Settings** — the ✨ Image Studio button only appears in chat once image generation is switched on, but that switch used to be tucked away inside the character creator — so it was easy to never realize the feature existed. There's now a clear **Image Generation** toggle in **Settings → Voice & Media**: flip it on and the Image Studio button shows up in your chat toolbar. (Backend, model and LoRA setup still live inside the Image Studio.)

- 🔁 **Windows updates now install right where the app already lives — even a custom folder** — after a recent change gave each channel its own install folder, the Nightly in-app updater started dropping a *second* copy into AppData and leaving the one you were running untouched — so the app never moved to the new version and kept nagging you to update over and over. Updates now install **in place, in the exact folder you originally chose** (any drive, any path), so the build you run is the one that gets updated and the loop is gone. Brand-new installs still let you **pick where to install** as before. (Windows only.)

- 🛠️ **Nightly builds now actually ship the latest code (this was the real cause of "missing features" + the endless update prompt)** — a caching step meant to speed up builds was accidentally caching the *whole* compiled app, not just the AI engine sidecars. So every nightly after a certain point re-shipped a **frozen older copy of the app** while stamping it with the current date — Windows builds were stuck on older code, macOS on a different older build. That's why fresh installs were missing recent features and the "Update Available" prompt never went away (the version baked into the app could never catch up to the release tag). Builds now compile and ship the app fresh on every run — the risky caching that caused this is gone entirely — so a nightly's in-app version matches its release and new features actually arrive. (All platforms.)

- 🍎 **macOS Nightly updates now actually replace the running app (no more endless "Update Available")** — the Nightly build shared its internal identity with the Stable build, so both `/Applications/FrontPorchAI-Rawhide.app` (Nightly) and the Stable app claimed the *same* bundle identifier. When you ran an update the macOS Installer couldn't tell which app to overwrite, so the new build never landed on the Nightly you were running — it stayed on the old version and kept re-prompting forever (the macOS twin of the Windows loop above). Nightly now ships with its **own** identity, so updates cleanly overwrite the Nightly app in place. One-time note: this changeover resets *Nightly's* app preferences once (your chats, characters and world data are untouched). (macOS Nightly only.)

- 🎨 **Generate character avatars on the local backend too** — In the AI Character Creator's Review step, "Generate Avatar" was hidden whenever your chat model was KoboldCpp — a leftover from when KoboldCpp was mistakenly treated as the image source. Avatars actually come from the image generator you pick in Image Studio (Draw Things / A1111 / a remote API), independent of your chat model — so generating one now works no matter which LLM backend you're on.

- 🎯 **Group characters now react to their *own* state, not a castmate's** — a behind-the-scenes mix-up meant a character's hidden prompt could be fed the *other* member's needs, mood, and relationship, and in shuffled turn order the needs/bond changes always landed on the first character instead of whoever actually spoke. Each speaker now reads and updates their own stats, every turn, in any turn order — so characters behave consistently with how *they* actually feel.

- 📊 **Needs changes now show under every group member's messages** — the little needs chips (Hunger ▴, Bladder ▴, etc.) under a reply only appeared for the first character to respond after you typed; everyone else's messages showed none, even though their needs were updating behind the scenes. Now every speaker — including auto-advanced turns, `/speak`, and director chime-ins — shows their own needs changes on their message, exactly like the first responder.

- 🧭 **Cleaner group sidebar** — the right-hand panel now leads with **Main Settings** (the per-character menu) and puts the **Group Settings** button right beneath it, *above* the cast list — so whole-group options come before per-character ones instead of being buried under the roster. Director/Verifier settings also no longer appear twice: they live solely in **Group Settings → Realism**, where they work for every member (the broken, first-character-only copy on the Needs tab is gone).

- 🎭 **Character expressions now work in group chats** — if you'd set up Expression Images for a character, they only showed in 1:1 chats; in a group everyone was stuck on their single portrait. Now each group member automatically **inherits the expression set from your library character of the same name** — so the same emotional faces you see in a solo chat play during group scenes too. You can also set them up **right from the group**: pick the character in the sidebar, open **Settings → Expression Images**, and the editor now clearly shows *whose* faces you're editing and saves them to that character so they show up immediately (and everywhere that character appears). Characters with no expressions just keep their single portrait, as before.

- 🧬 **Evolve the character you actually picked** — opening Character Evolution in a group used to show whoever was next to speak, not the character you'd selected in the sidebar. Now it follows your selection, and in a group it has a **member-picker at the top** so you can view or manually evolve *any* cast member. Evolution also no longer re-fires on a regenerate (it was double-counting), and it's been told to refine rather than pile on — so characters stop endlessly repeating the same self-coined phrases.

- 🤝 **Trust actually moves now** — the trust meter was so conservative it basically never budged. It now responds to everyday trustworthiness — honesty, consistency, showing up, small kindnesses — not only grand gestures, while big betrayals still land hard.

- 🪪 **Characters use your name** — if a character kept calling you "the boy" / "the girl" instead of your name, the prompt now always establishes your name (even with a blank persona) and nudges the model to use it. (It won't be 100% — a literary model will still reach for the occasional epithet — but it's much better.)

- 🔀 **`/turnorder` — control who speaks when in a group** — tired of characters answering in a random order? In any group chat, type **`/turnorder roundrobin`** for a predictable cycle, **`/turnorder random`** to shuffle, or set an exact sequence with **`/turnorder Mara, Kai, Rin`** (anyone you leave out is added to the end, so nobody drops out of the rotation). `/turnorder` on its own shows the current mode and order. No more spamming `/speak` to wrangle the order.

- 🔥 **NSFW Enhancement (arousal) now works in group chats** — the arousal/lust toggle used to only exist in 1:1 chats, so in a group there was literally no way to turn it on and arousal never tracked. It now appears in the group sidebar, and flipping it correctly applies to **every** member (not just one), so lust starts moving for the whole cast.

- 🕯️ **Scene time no longer resets when you turn a 1:1 into a group** — converting a solo chat to a group (or collapsing back) used to snap a fixed scene time like "evening, Day 1" back to "morning, Day 1". Now the time of day and day count carry across the conversion intact, even with passage-of-time turned off.

- 💾 **Smarter automatic backups** — your database is now backed up every 30 minutes and kept on a two-tier rolling schedule: the most recent snapshots *plus* one backup per day for the last 7 days. So whether you need to undo something from this afternoon or roll back to yesterday's data, there's a restore point waiting — and old ones are pruned automatically so they never pile up. Manage and restore them on the Backups screen.

- ⚠️ **Cloud Sync is deprecated** — Cloud Sync has proven unreliable across devices (it could even bring deleted characters back to life), so it's now marked deprecated and **will be removed in a future release**. It still runs for now, but the supported way forward is the improved local backups above for safekeeping, and **Card export / import** to move characters or groups between devices. A clear notice now appears on the Cloud Sync screen.

- 🧬 **Turning a 1:1 into a group (and back) now keeps everything** — converting a solo chat to a group, or collapsing a group back to a 1:1, used to quietly reset a bunch of your character's state. Now it all carries across, both ways: the relationship/mood/needs, **character evolution** (their developed personality & scenario), **active quests/objectives**, **author's notes**, the **NSFW-enhancement toggle and arousal**, chaos mode, passage-of-time, and the AI's **deep memory** of the conversation — so after a conversion the cast can still recall specific earlier events that have scrolled off-screen, in *both* directions. Your character keeps who they've become no matter how the scene's cast changes. (One small caveat: a group's evolution *counter* still resets if you fully close and reopen the group before collapsing — the evolved traits themselves are kept.)

- 🗣️ **`/speak <name>` now works in full groups** — the macro that makes a present character jump in and take their turn right now isn't just for Lite NPCs anymore. In a full group chat, type **`/speak Mara`** and Mara takes the next turn immediately instead of waiting for the rotation (partial names work too; an unknown or ambiguous name tells you who's actually in the scene).

- 🔗 **Exported Group Cards remember each member's original character** — when you export a group and later re-import it, the members now reconnect to the matching characters in *your* library instead of arriving as untraceable copies. So if you ever shrink that re-imported group back down to a 1:1, it lands on the real library character. (Older cards still import fine; they just fall back to matching by name.)

- 👥 **`/join` and `/exit` now work inside full group chats too** — the cast macros are no longer 1:1-only. In a group, type **`/join Mara`** to bring a character in as a full member (no `--full` needed — a group has no "lite" tier, so it's always full), and **`/exit Mara`** to remove a member. When someone leaves they now **say a proper goodbye in their own voice** instead of just vanishing — and an **UNDO** appears for a few seconds: tap it and the goodbye is removed and they're back *exactly* as they were, with every bit of their bond, mood, needs, evolution, quests and memory intact (nothing is actually deleted until you carry on). Remove members until only one is left and the chat **automatically collapses back into a clean 1:1** with that original character — no leftover duplicate copies. The old "Add Character" button is gone from the group sidebar (freeing up space); just type `/` to see every command and pick `/join`.

- 🔄 **Redo your main character's reply even after a Scene Guest has answered** — when a Lite NPC chimes in right after your main character, you used to be stuck: the regenerate button only ever lived on the *very last* message, so once the guest spoke you could no longer redo the main character's response. Now a regenerate icon appears on the main character's bubble too. Tap it and the guest's now-stale reply is cleared away, your main character's response is rolled back and regenerated (relationships, mood and Needs revert correctly, exactly like a normal redo), and then the guest decides for itself whether to chime in again — speaking up only if the new reply actually concerns it, instead of blindly repeating.

- 🪟 **One chat screen for everyone — solo, guests, and groups** — single chats, 1:1 chats with Scene Guests, and full group chats now all use the *exact same* screen, header, and sidebar (the SillyTavern way). A solo chat is simply a cast of one; add a guest or another character and the same window grows a little **roster of avatars** at the top of the sidebar — tap any face to focus that character and see *their* emotion, bond, trust, needs, fixation and growth. The header shows a single portrait for a solo chat and a neat stack of portraits once there's more than one speaker. Nothing about your existing chats changes under the hood — your solo characters' relationships and Needs behave exactly as before; this just unifies the look and lets you move smoothly between a quiet 1:1 and a crowded scene without ever switching screens.

- ⌨️ **Type `/` to see every chat command** — no more memorizing the macros. The moment you type `/` in the message box, a little list pops up right above it showing every command (`/create`, `/join`, `/speak`, `/exit`, `/scan`, `/expression`) with a one-line description of what each does; it filters as you keep typing (`/sp` → just `/speak`), and you can **tap any one to drop it into the box** ready for you to fill in. It tucks itself away as soon as you start typing your actual message.

- 👋 **Invite your existing characters into a 1:1 chat with `/join`** — Scene Guests aren't limited to made-on-the-spot newcomers anymore. Type `/join` to pop up a searchable picker of your whole character library and tap whoever you want to walk into the scene — or type `/join Mara` to bring them in instantly by name (it opens the picker if the name is ambiguous). Whoever you pick makes a natural entrance written from the current conversation and their own character card, then sticks around in their own bubble and speaks for themselves, just like a `/create`d guest. The picker never lists your current main character (you can't invite the host into their own chat) or anyone already in the scene. And because they join as a *guest*, none of their own Realism, mood, or Needs comes along — your main character's simulation stays untouched. Mix and match freely: several guests can share a scene, each taking their own turns and chiming in when it's natural. Want a specific guest to speak *right now* instead of waiting for them to chime in? Type **`/speak <name>`** (or just `/speak` for the most-recent guest) — and if you mistype the name, it tells you who's actually in the scene. And if you `/exit` a guest by mistake, an **UNDO** appears for a few seconds: tap it and the goodbye message is removed and the guest is back exactly as they were — same memories, same personality growth.

- 🧠 **Scene Guests now remember** — the lite characters who drop into your 1:1 chats finally have a memory of their own. When a guest takes a turn, Front Porch AI quietly files away what just happened under *that guest's* name, and on their later turns it recalls the relevant past moments — so a returning guest can pick up threads from earlier in the scene (and even from other chats they've been in) instead of acting like they just walked in. Each guest's memories stay theirs: your main character's recollections never leak onto a guest, and a guest's never bleed onto your main. (Uses your existing RAG memory setting — nothing new to turn on.)

- 🌱 **Scene Guests now grow on you** — the lite characters you drop into a 1:1 chat no longer stay flat. As a guest keeps showing up and taking turns, Front Porch AI quietly lets them develop — picking up new traits and reflecting how the scene has shifted, exactly the way your main characters do with Character Evolution. Their growth sticks to that conversation, takes effect on their later turns, and never touches your main character's mood, relationship, or Needs. (Character Evolution must be enabled in settings; guests evolve on the same "every N messages" cadence as everyone else.)

- 🪄 **Scene Guests now arrive on their own** — when your main character keeps mentioning the same new named person in their narration (a sister, a regular at the bar, a recurring rival), Front Porch AI quietly notices and pops up a gentle "“Mara” has joined your chat with Aria." offer. One tap on **Add as Scene Guest** turns them into a real, self-speaking character (using the same instant generation as `/create`); tap **Ignore** and they stay background narration and won't be offered again. It only watches your 1:1 chats, checks in occasionally (not every line), and never suggests your own character or someone already in the scene — so it stays out of the way until there's genuinely a new face worth bringing to life. In a hurry (or testing it on a chat where a side character already keeps coming up)? Type **`/scan`** to run the check right now instead of waiting for the next automatic pass.

- 🎭 **Scene Guests — drop a second character into a 1:1 chat** — type `/create Mara: a wandering bard` mid-conversation and a brand-new character is generated on the spot, joins your scene in their *own* message bubble with their own name and avatar, and speaks for themselves through the same AI engine. They become a real, saved library character (so you can reuse them later). Use `/exit` (or `/exit Mara`) and your main character narrates them leaving. Scene Guests are intentionally lightweight: they don't carry Realism, mood, or Needs tracking, so adding one never disturbs your main character's relationship, emotions, or simulation.
